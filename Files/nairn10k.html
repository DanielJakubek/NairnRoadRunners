Nairn 10k


Nairn 10k Results 2024
Enter Nairn 10k 2024

The course is an SAF measured course. There is one water station on the route at approx. 7.25k on 10k route. The race itself starts and finishes on the Nairn High Street which itself is closed to cars for the race. The rest of the course is a fast open course which leaves the town and winds its way back in with few turns in it and has a fast finish down to the main High Street where it finishes under the Town Clock.

Entry

£17 for non-affiliated runners (including Jog Scotland), £15 for Scottish Athletics affiliated runners. The price shown includes all fees and charges. Scottish Athletics, British Athletics/UKA and Welsh Athletics members can obtain the discount by entering their membership number into the appropriate field during the registration process.

Registration

From 13:00pm – 14:00pm on race day at the Nairn Community Centre where you will be issued with your race number.

Safety

Please note that the race mainly takes place on roads that remain open to traffic. All runners are responsible for their own safety and must ensure that they remain aware of other road users at all times and can clearly hear instructions from marshals and other race officials. In accordance with UK Athletic rules, the wearing of headphones are not permitted, anyone found wearing these may be disqualified from the race. Please obey course marshal’s instructions and directions.

Medals

Each 10k finisher will receive a medal. Prizes sponsored by Hamish Property professionals will be awarded in the Nairn community centre for top three male and female runners.

  • 1st £250
  • 2nd £150
  • 3rd £100
  • Age group 1st £30

Fun run

Fun runners can register on the day at a cost of £5 in the Nairn Community centre. There are limited spaces for this event. Registration will close at 2:00pm prompt. Noted this event will not be timed on the day and all runners will be awarded medals.

Minimum age is 9. All under age of 10 must be accompanied by an adult during the fun run as this takes place on open roads Nairn Road Runners will not be responsible for their safety.
